·Rummenigge leaves Bayern

Karl-Heinz-Rummenigge will step down as CEO of Bayern at the end of June. The club announced it a few minutes ago on his website. Another legend like Oliver Kahn will succeed him in office. "This is the most strategically sensible and logical moment," says Karl-Heinz Rummenigge. “It is the end of the financial year and a new section begins with a new team of coaches. Oliver Kahn, as the new CEO, should be responsible for the new season from the beginning, also in the interest of the people involved and therefore in the interest of the future of FC Bayern. Two years ago I said that we were planning a prudent transition, and now it is finally being done. This is how it should be, ”the statement said.
6/1/21 Source: Onefootball

·The happiness of Bayer Leverkusen for the Champions of Kai Havertz

Faster than expected. A year after Havertz left Bayer 04, he rose to the throne of European football. Although the beginning of his adventure in English football was anything but ideal. Havertz got used to the bank and, to top it all, contracted COVID-19. Everything changed with the arrival of Thomas Tuchel, where together with Timo Werner, he began to start and play a fundamental role in the team. His peak came last Saturday in the Porto final when he scored his first goal in the top European competition and gave Chelsea the second Champions League. At Leverkusen, the match was followed with a special interest in him. And the sporting director, Simon Rolfes, could not be more proud: "We are very happy for him and we are proud that everything has gone this way. The game and the goal were his crowning glory"
